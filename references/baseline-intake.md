# Operating baseline and traction intake

Ask only the questions that change a decision. Product/business and finance owners submit facts in separate packs. The Agent, not either owner, classifies stage, Ansoff quadrant, and growth curve.

## Selection rule

Ask at most six first-round questions per owner. Use factual questions, not framework labels.

### Product/business owner pack

Ask for:

1. own historical product, user, channel, region, price, volume, quality, service, and trust performance;
2. market size/growth/profit-pool evidence, source, and date;
3. competitor and best-alternative performance, positioning, price, channel, and user evidence;
4. user problem, willingness to pay, adoption, retention, and switching evidence;
5. capabilities, control points, delivery constraints, and failed attempts;
6. proposed opportunities, what will not be done, and why.

### Finance owner pack

Ask for:

1. comparable P&L and cash baselines;
2. price-volume-mix-channel-region growth bridge;
3. healthy gross margin, operating profit, and unit-efficiency bridge;
4. inventory, collection, working capital, and irreversible commitments;
5. investment, budget ceiling, downside exposure, and payback/stop threshold;
6. AI package/human package baseline, actual workflow adoption, and measurable result.

For a cross-circle review, add a boundary pack: result ownership, product/price/channel overlap, shared capabilities, cannibalization, price-integrity, lifecycle, and exit rules.

For every answer require metric definition, baseline period/value, source, owner, target period/value, gap, causal driver, and update date.

## Universal evidence checks

1. What are the comparable prior full-year actual, prior H2 actual, current H1 actual/latest forecast, and current H2 budget for revenue, gross profit, gross margin, operating profit, and cash?
2. Which customer, product, channel, region, price, and volume changes explain growth or decline? Separate facts from management targets.
3. What is unit efficiency (`gross profit / controllable operating expense`), and what changes it by each BP half-year?
4. Which trust, quality, service, delivery, inventory, collection, or compliance indicator can block the financial target?
5. What is the AI package / human package baseline, which workflow is adopted, and what measurable revenue, margin, cash, cycle, quality, or capacity result does it create?
6. Which subject does each number represent: circle revenue, regional/full-market revenue, sell-in/SI, sell-out/SO, recognized management revenue, operating profit, net profit variant, contribution margin, or cash? What evidence converts a leading indicator into a result?

## Questions by business type

### Consumer product or product line

- What are domestic/overseas, channel, price-band, SKU, and new-product revenue and gross-margin baselines?
- What share comes from premium products, and what proves willingness to pay rather than discount-driven volume?
- What are conversion, return, defect, service-cost, recommendation, and trust baselines?
- Which SKU/channel/market will be exited or deprioritized to fund the selected opportunities?
- If multiple circles or series share a price band, what user/value boundary prevents cannibalization and which circle owns the result?

### Commercial B2B

- What are pipeline, win rate, sales cycle, backlog, project gross margin, delivery cycle, collection period, and top-customer concentration?
- What share comes from KA, major channels, overseas, new customers, and repeat customers?
- Which projects destroy margin or cash, and what bid/no-bid rule will stop them?
- How does R&D or solution investment map to a named product, customer, market, and payment milestone?

### ODM or subsidiary

- What are domestic/overseas, existing/new customer, old/new category, and top-customer revenue/margin mixes?
- What are capacity utilization, yield, procurement, inventory, receivables, tooling, warranty, and customer-concentration baselines?
- Which customer/category expansion improves margin and cash instead of only volume?
- What is the minimum viable revenue and net-margin floor for staying in the business?

### Platform or function

- Which SP opportunities and BP results depend on this capability?
- What are current cycle, quality, reuse, adoption, SLA, error/rework, and capacity baselines?
- What downstream revenue, gross profit, cash release, loss avoidance, or risk reduction can be attributed without double counting?
- Which business owner commits to adoption, and what adoption threshold releases the next budget?
- If multiple functions or circles claim the same downstream result, what attribution and ownership rule prevents double counting?

### Company portfolio

- What is the opportunity and investment mix by market/technology drive, Ansoff quadrant, and first/second/third curve?
- What is the actual `80/20` and `70/20/10` denominator after exclusions, and why does any exception exist?
- Which three company opportunities receive resources, and which opportunities explicitly lose or wait?
- What are portfolio revenue, healthy margin, cash, trust, talent-density, and AI leverage baselines?

### Cross-circle or cross-channel review

- Who owns each product, customer, channel, price band, and result? Which claims are shared, and how is double counting prevented?
- Where do product series or circles overlap in user, scenario, price, channel, or capability? Which authority resolves the overlap?
- Which channel/customer requests are evidence inputs rather than approved strategic choices, and what is the explicit sacrifice?
- What are the price-integrity, cannibalization, lifecycle, and exit rules for exclusive or channel-specific versions?

## Agent stage classification

Do not ask “which stage are you in?” Infer it from evidence:

| Stage | Evidence pattern |
|---|---|
| Survival | cash or loss threatens continuity; break-even and minimum viable portfolio dominate |
| Turnaround | established business is declining or structurally unprofitable and must repair/exit |
| Growth | demand and economics have evidence, but repeatability or capacity is not yet mature |
| Scale | E4-like repeatability, operating leverage, quality, and delivery support expansion |
| Incubation | new opportunity remains E0-E2 and primarily purchases learning |
| Transformation | the primary change is process/platform/organization migration and adoption |

If evidence matches two stages, name the dominant stage and the secondary transition. State confidence and the missing fact that would change the classification.

## Questions by stage

| Stage | Ask for these baselines and traction metrics |
|---|---|
| Survival | cash runway, break-even point, loss sources, minimum viable portfolio, fixed/variable cost, collections, irreversible commitments |
| Turnaround | decline decomposition, loss-making products/customers, gross-margin repair, cash release, exit milestones, owner and deadline |
| Growth | growth source, marginal gross margin, CAC/payback or sales efficiency, channel capacity, delivery bottleneck, working-capital need |
| Scale | repeatability, operating leverage, quality escape, concentration, inventory, service capacity, international compliance and localization |
| Incubation | problem evidence, experiment conversion, willingness to pay, option cost, learning milestone, expiry/kill date |
| Transformation | current process cost/cycle/quality, adoption, migration cost, displaced work, benefit realization, human accountability |

## Questions by Ansoff quadrant

| Quadrant | Required baseline and traction questions |
|---|---|
| Existing product / existing market | share, penetration, conversion, retention, price/mix, channel efficiency, incremental margin, cannibalization |
| Existing product / new market | local demand, compliance, localization, channel economics, service coverage, CAC/payback, trust and payment evidence |
| New product / existing market | user problem, attach rate, willingness to pay, cannibalization, development/tooling cost, defect/service cost, target gross margin |
| New product / new market | problem evidence, reachable market, unit-economics range, option budget, capability gaps, partner dependency, expiry/exit date |

The Agent determines the quadrant from whether the actual product and actual target market are already proven. Do not accept the owner's label without checking. If “existing” is disputed, state which product/market evidence is missing.

## Questions by growth curve

- First curve: What current P&L, cash, efficiency, product, channel, and trust baseline must be defended or upgraded?
- Second curve: What evidence proves repeatability, when does it become material, and what capability/control point supports migration?
- Third curve: What learning is being purchased, what is the option ceiling, when does the option expire, and what kills it?

The Agent determines the curve from current economic contribution, evidence maturity, and migration role—not from the project name or sponsor.

## Conflict reconciliation

When owner packs disagree, return:

| Metric/claim | Product/business input | Finance input | Definition/source difference | Decision impact | Resolver/date |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Do not average, select the more convenient figure, or delay exposing the conflict.

## Baseline request output

Return one table:

| Metric | Why it matters | Definition | Baseline period/value | Source/owner | Target period/value | Gap/driver | Due date |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

If a value is unavailable, write `GAP` and keep the owner and due date. Never fill it with an estimate unless the owner explicitly marks it as an `ASSUMPTION`.
