# SOUL decision kernel template

This public file is a configurable template. It deliberately contains no company name, internal document link, access token, private target, or current-period commitment. Before a review, load the latest approved SOUL or decision kernel supplied by the organization under review. If that source is unavailable, mark the affected claims `GAP` and use preliminary mode.

## Configuration

Replace these placeholders with the approved planning cycle:

- SP cycle: `<SP start>—<SP end>`
- BP cycle: `<BP start>—<BP end>`
- Current budget cycle: `<budget period>`
- SP milestones: every approved half-year milestone in the SP cycle
- BP milestones: every approved half-year milestone in the BP cycle
- Decision authorities: named strategy, product, finance, delivery, and red-line owners

Do not infer dates, thresholds, or authorities from this template.

## Stable decision kernel

The organization’s SOUL should define the long-term mission, vision, worldview, values, talent view, and non-tradable red lines. For SP/BP review, translate it into testable rules:

- safety, integrity, privacy, quality, and compliance are not tradable;
- unique user/customer value is the investment ticket;
- market and user evidence come first; technology options are bounded and time-boxed;
- adjacent growth needs less evidence than a move across product, market, or capability boundaries;
- premium positioning, healthy margin, cash, delivery quality, and long-term trust must reinforce one another;
- budget is released in stages as evidence matures; irreversible investment requires the organization’s defined E3-equivalent evidence gate or stronger.

If the SOUL defines product or service principles (for example, a “protect rather than monitor” principle), quote the approved wording in the review record and test the plan against it. Do not replace the organization’s wording with this template.

## Unique value test

Unique value must show:

1. horizontal difference versus industry products and the best alternative;
2. vertical progress versus the organization’s prior offer or capability;
3. user-perceived value that changes choice, trust, willingness to pay, adoption, or retention.

A channel request, executive sponsorship, sunk cost, or a polished deck is not unique-value evidence.

## Evidence maturity

| Level | Meaning | Normally allowed |
|---|---|---|
| E0 | opportunity hypothesis only | research and low-cost problem validation |
| E1 | real problem and demand validated | concept and target-user validation |
| E2 | solution and technical feasibility validated | prototype, sample, limited pilot |
| E3 | critical next-irreversible-investment evidence is closed across demand value, solution/technology, GTM/adoption, and delivery/trust | conditional tooling, production, procurement, or channel expansion |
| E4 | repeatable growth and scale conditions validated | scaled allocation with marginal-return monitoring |

## Decision chain

`SWOT/TOWS → Ansoff → growth curve → SOUL hard gates → E0-E4 → staged budget → review and reallocation`

## Verdicts

- `通过`: evidence supports the approved execution scale.
- `验证`: direction is plausible; release validation budget only.
- `暂停`: critical evidence, capability, delivery condition, or ownership is missing.
- `终止`: a red line is hit, unique value is absent, or commercial closure is disproven.

## Decision authority

Use the organization’s actual names and mandates. At minimum, identify:

- business/product owner for evidence and results;
- strategy/portfolio body for choices and resource allocation;
- product/technology body for unique value and irreversible product gates;
- finance controller for margin, cash, and staged budget release;
- delivery/capacity owner for shared resource conflicts;
- safety, quality, privacy, and compliance owners for red-line vetoes;
- final executive decision owner for strategy and major exceptions.
