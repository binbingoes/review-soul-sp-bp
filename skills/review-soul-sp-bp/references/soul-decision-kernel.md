# SOUL template: stable core and SP strategic pull

This public template contains only the long-term SOUL core and qualitative SP strategic pull. It deliberately excludes BP periods, budget periods, target numbers, quotas, financial thresholds, release amounts, and dated operating commitments. Read the bundled `goal.md` for all quantitative SP/BP/budget goals and gates.

If the organization has an approved private SOUL, load that version first. Replace this template only with approved wording; do not infer a company mission, vision, target, or authority from the public file.

## SOUL core

The organization’s SOUL defines its mission, vision, worldview, values, talent view, and non-tradable red lines. For smart-hardware and AI-hardware companies, the core should make trust, safety, privacy, reliability, and responsible use explicit.

## SP strategic pull

Use the SP layer to make durable choices about where the company will create value and what it will refuse to do. The SP pull should answer:

- Which user, household, professional, or care scenario deserves focused attention?
- What distinctive device-plus-AI experience changes user choice, trust, willingness to pay, or adoption?
- What hardware, embedded intelligence, data, service, or ecosystem control point makes the value defensible?
- Which adjacent arena is the next credible move, and which farther moves remain options rather than commitments?
- How will premium value, healthy economics, cash discipline, delivery quality, and long-term trust reinforce one another?
- What explicit sacrifice protects product focus, price integrity, privacy, reliability, and service quality?

## Smart-hardware / AI-hardware principles

Use these as a review lens, not as a substitute for the organization’s approved wording:

- Protect rather than monitor; connect without disturbing.
- An alert or prediction is not the result; a safe, useful closed loop is the result.
- AI should help people care, decide, and act better; it should not erase human responsibility.
- Device, embedded model, cloud/service, installation, and after-sales experience must be designed as one trusted product system.
- Safety, privacy, security, firmware reliability, certification, and serviceability are product value—not post-launch paperwork.

## Qualitative SP gates

1. Red lines are not tradable: safety, integrity, privacy, security, quality, certification, and compliance.
2. Distinctive user value is the investment ticket.
3. Market/user evidence leads; technology options are bounded and falsifiable.
4. Adjacent growth needs less evidence than a move across product, market, price, channel, or capability boundaries.
5. The chosen strategy must strengthen trust, healthy economics, delivery, and capability—not only shipment volume.
6. Irreversible commitments require the evidence maturity and authority defined in `goal.md`.

## Evidence maturity lens

Use E0-E4 to describe how much of the SP choice is evidenced. The exact milestone dates, quantitative thresholds, validation budget, release condition, and stop condition belong in `goal.md`, not in SOUL.

| Level | Meaning |
|---|---|
| E0 | opportunity hypothesis only |
| E1 | real problem and demand signal validated |
| E2 | solution, device, and technical feasibility validated |
| E3 | critical next-commitment evidence closed across demand, solution, GTM/adoption, and delivery/trust |
| E4 | repeatable growth, quality, service, and economics validated |
