# Strategy framework execution rules for smart hardware and AI hardware

Use frameworks to select and falsify choices, not to decorate a presentation.

## 1. SWOT and TOWS

For every item require `fact`, `source`, `date`, and `decision implication`.

- Strength: an internal capability proven relative to the target arena.
- Weakness: an internal constraint that changes feasibility or economics.
- Opportunity: an external shift with a reachable user, market, or profit pool.
- Threat: an external force that can destroy value, access, margin, or trust.

Generate options:

- SO: use a strength to capture an opportunity.
- WO: close a weakness to capture an opportunity.
- ST: use a strength to reduce a threat.
- WT: avoid, exit, partner, or buy an option when weakness meets threat.

Reject SWOT items that are adjectives, unsupported beliefs, or duplicates of the desired conclusion.

## 2. Ansoff matrix

| Quadrant | Product | Market | Default evidence implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Market penetration | existing | existing | prove incremental demand, share capture, margin, and channel economics |
| Market development | existing | new | revalidate local need, compliance, channel, service, and trust |
| Product development | new | existing | revalidate unique value, willingness to pay, product economics, and delivery |
| Diversification | new | new | smallest initial budget, strongest evidence bar, explicit option expiry |

Do not reuse product evidence as proof of a new market, or channel evidence as proof of a new product.

## 3. Growth curves

- First curve: improve and defend the current economic engine.
- Second curve: build a new engine with evidence of future repeatability.
- Third curve: buy a bounded future option; do not pre-fund scale.

Require for each curve:

1. starting evidence level;
2. half-year migration milestones;
3. capability and control point;
4. budget ceiling and stop condition;
5. trigger for promotion, continued validation, pause, or exit.

## 4. Where to Play and How to Win

Where to Play must select:

- user/customer;
- job or scenario;
- geography/channel;
- category/product boundary;
- price/profit pool;
- explicit non-choice.

How to Win must state:

- differentiated promise;
- core control point or defensible capability;
- product, GTM, delivery, and trust mechanism;
- result metric and owner;
- competitor or alternative response;
- what evidence would falsify the strategy.

If a plan cannot name what it will not do, mark strategic choice as incomplete.

## 5. Portfolio and finance link

Connect each opportunity to:

- revenue source and timing;
- healthy gross margin and operating-profit path;
- cash use, inventory, collection, and irreversible commitments;
- unit-efficiency improvement;
- trust and delivery obligations;
- AI Native leverage and actual workflow adoption;
- SP milestone, BP milestone, current budget, next gate, and stop condition.

A growth target without a causal bridge from actions to revenue, margin, cash, and trust is an assumption, not a plan.

## 6. Hardware system lens

Apply the strategy frameworks to the full product system:

`user scenario → device interaction → embedded intelligence → cloud/service → installation → after-sales → trust`

For each selected opportunity, identify the bottleneck and control point across:

- device differentiation, sensors/actuators, embedded model, and human override;
- BOM, tooling, yield, certification, firmware/OTA, and production quality;
- activation, adoption, model quality, failure/defect, privacy/security, and service SLA;
- lifecycle economics: gross margin, cloud/model/API cost, warranty, returns, inventory, collection, and support;
- channel price integrity, cannibalization, lifecycle ownership, and ecosystem dependency.

Do not call a hardware strategy validated from shipment, channel commitment, demo quality, or model accuracy alone. Require the user outcome and the safe, reliable, economically viable closed loop.
